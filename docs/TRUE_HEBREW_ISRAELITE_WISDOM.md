## The Framer and The Shaper (Tepeu and Gucumatz)

### From the Popol Vuh (The Book of the People)

The Popol Vuh is the foundational sacred narrative of the K'iche' Maya people, preserved through oral tradition since long before the Spanish conquest. It tells of creation by **Tepeu** (God of the Skies) and **Gucumatz** (God of the Seas/Feathered Serpent) - known together as **The Framer and The Shaper**.

> "Tepeu and Gucumatz talked together. As they spoke, they agreed and created mountains, rivers, valleys and the rest of the Earth."

### Parallels to Genesis

The Popol Vuh opens remarkably similar to the Hebrew Bible's Genesis:
- Creation from pre-world materials
- Waters covering the earth before land
- Multiple attempts to create humans (earth/mud, then wood, finally maize/corn)
- Divine council speaking creation into existence ("Let there be...")
- Humans created from the earth itself

The K'iche' word structure and creation narrative bears striking resemblance to Hebrew cosmology - evidence that the TRUE Hebrew-Israelites carried this knowledge across the waters.

Tecumseh and his brother **Tenskwatawa (The Prophet)** were visionaries who called for ALL Native peoples to unite as ONE PEOPLE against the encroaching colonizers.

On **August 11, 1802**, Tecumseh prophesied that the ground would shake. On **December 16, 1811**, the **New Madrid Earthquake** struck - one of the most powerful earthquakes in American history - fulfilling his prophecy.

The Prophet called for an eclipse of the sun to prove his connection to the Creator - and it came.
